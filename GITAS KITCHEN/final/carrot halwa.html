Gajar Ka Halwa | Carrot Halwa With Milkmaid

Making the popular Indian dessert known as Carrot Halwa (a.k.a. Gajar Ka Halwa in Hindi or Gajrela in Punjabi) is so simple when you use my detailed step-by-step guide and video! Whether you make it using the traditional stovetop method, or take some shortcuts by using milkmaid, khoya or the Instant Pot, I have you covered all with detailed step-by-step photos in this helpful post.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

1 hr.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Desserts

instructions

step 1

Melt 6 tablespoons ghee in a heavy kadai or thick bottomed skillet or pan. Add in 9 cups of tightly packed grated carrots.

step 2

Sauté the carrots until they turn tender and are cooked, about 15 to 20 minutes on low heat. Stir often.

step 3

Pour in an entire can of milkmaid or sweetened condensed milk (400 grams).

step 4

Mix thoroughly.

step 5

Add 1.5 to 2 teaspoons cardamom powder, ⅓ cup raisins and ⅓ cup cashews.

step 6

Mix well and simmer gajar ka halwa on a low heat. Stir often at intervals until the mixture thickens, reduces and dries up.

step 7

Serve carrot halwa hot or warm or chilled.

ingredients

1.5 kg carrots – approximate 9 cups tightly packed grated carrots

6 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)

1.5 to 2 teaspoons cardamom powder or 8 to 9 green cardamoms – powdered in a mortar-pestle

1 tin sweetened condensed milk – I used nestle milkmaid, 400 grams

sugar as required, optional

⅓ cup cashews

⅓ cup golden raisins

tools

Kadai

Spatula
